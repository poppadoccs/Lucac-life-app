## Research subject

Research how Lucac should explain its delivery process so clients trust how the
work gets done. The point is to remove uncertainty for a trades owner who has
been burned by vague "web guys" before.

Include:
- onboarding process for each service (website, chatbot, email setup, email
  migration, automations, Google Business setup, custom apps, audit/takeover,
  care plans)
- what access clients need to provide (domain, DNS, hosting, email, GBP, etc.)
- what Lucac checks before launch
- what handoff includes
- care plan onboarding (what the first 30 days look like)
- revision vs. new work (where revisions stop and a change order starts)
- change order language (plain-spoken)
- what "done" means by service
- first-call script outline
- what should happen after an inquiry (the steps from inquiry to kickoff)

## Required output

- **Process section / page recommendation** — whether to add a "How it works"
  page or section, and what it contains.
- **Onboarding checklist** — concrete steps, reusable across services with
  per-service notes.
- **Launch checklist** — what Lucac verifies before going live.
- **Handoff checklist** — exactly what the client receives at handoff (logins,
  docs, training, ownership).
- **Done criteria by service** — a clear definition of "done" for each service.
- **Client-access checklist** — the exact access/credentials to request per
  service.
- **Simple scope language** — plain-spoken revision vs. new-work / change-order
  wording suitable for the website and proposals.
