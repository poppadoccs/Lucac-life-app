# Research Prompt 01 — Competitor Positioning

## Role

You are a research analyst helping upgrade **lucacllc.com**. Research only — do
not implement, build, or edit anything. Use current web research and cite your
sources.

## About Lucac LLC

Lucac LLC is a small service-business tech company for contractors, trades, local
service businesses, and serious small businesses.

Services offered:
- Websites
- AI intake chatbots
- Business email setup
- Email migration
- Email automations
- Google Business Profile setup
- Custom apps
- Existing system audits / takeovers
- Monthly care plans

**Founder differentiator:** 18 years in construction/trades. Plain-spoken.
Practical. Not corporate. Not generic AI hype.

## Current goal

We are upgrading lucacllc.com into a top-tier, complete, trust-building website.
Your research must directly help improve the website — not become abstract
strategy.

## Rules for this research

- Research only. No implementation.
- Use current web research and cite sources.
- Prioritize practical recommendations we can apply to the website.
- Every recommendation must become one of these:
  - a page to add
  - a section to add
  - copy to change
  - metadata/schema to add
  - a backend/security improvement
  - a tracking event
  - a trust signal
  - a risk disclaimer
  - a specific implementation checklist item
- No generic advice.
- Keep Lucac plain-spoken, trades-friendly, and practical.
- Avoid fake proof, fake testimonials, guaranteed leads, guaranteed rankings,
  unlimited support, same-day fix promises, or AI hype.

## Research subject

Research Florida / Central Florida web agencies, local SEO providers, automation
consultants, AI chatbot sellers, MSPs (managed service providers), and website
care/maintenance providers.

**Goal:** figure out how Lucac can look different and stronger.

Research each competitor type for:
- competitor services (what they sell, how they bundle it)
- pricing if public (note when it is hidden behind "contact us")
- homepage positioning (their headline, sub-headline, primary promise)
- proof / case studies (what real proof they show vs. vague claims)
- CTA strategy (what action they push, where, how aggressively)
- trust signals (badges, reviews, guarantees, credentials, local proof)
- generic language to avoid (the tired phrases everyone in this space uses)
- how Lucac can use the 18-years-in-trades angle better than any of them

## Required output

- **Competitor table** — columns: name, type, services, public pricing,
  homepage headline, proof shown, primary CTA, trust signals, notable weakness.
- **Positioning gaps** — specific openings Lucac can own that competitors leave
  empty (each gap tied to a page or section we'd add/change).
- **Best Lucac positioning** — the single clearest position to take, in
  plain-spoken trades language, with the reasoning.
- **5 hero headline options** — ready-to-use headlines for the homepage hero,
  trades-friendly, no hype, no guarantees.
- **What not to copy** — the generic phrasing, fake proof patterns, and
  hype/guarantee claims competitors use that Lucac must avoid.

## Final output requirements (every prompt)

Do not implement anything.

Your final answer must end with:

1. Highest-priority changes for lucacllc.com
2. Specific website pages/sections affected
3. Exact copy or checklist items where possible
4. What is known
5. What is likely
6. What still needs verification
