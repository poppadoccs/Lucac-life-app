## Research subject

Research how Lucac can be easier for AI search and answer engines to understand
and cite accurately.

Include:
- ChatGPT Search
- OpenAI crawlers (GPTBot, OAI-SearchBot, ChatGPT-User) — current names/behavior
- Bing / Copilot
- Perplexity (PerplexityBot and any user-agent behavior)
- Claude-related crawling if applicable (ClaudeBot / anthropic-ai)
- Google AI results (AI Overviews / answer features)
- llms.txt and llms-full.txt — what they are, current adoption, who actually
  reads them, and whether they're worth adding
- answer-style service page formatting (clear questions/answers, definitions,
  plain summaries the engines can lift)
- robots.txt crawler allow/disallow recommendations for AI bots specifically

Be explicit about what is **proven to work** vs. **experimental/speculative** so
we don't waste effort on unproven tactics.

## Required output

- **AI discoverability checklist** — concrete site changes, ordered by
  confidence/impact.
- **robots.txt draft** — including explicit allow/disallow lines for the AI
  crawlers above, with a one-line rationale per bot.
- **llms.txt draft** — a ready-to-use draft for Lucac (services, pricing
  summary, contact, key pages).
- **llms-full.txt outline** — section-by-section outline of what the longer
  file should contain.
- **What is proven vs experimental** — a two-column split so we know what's
  safe to rely on.
- **AI-summarizable copy / FAQ blocks** — example question/answer blocks (in
  Lucac's voice) that AI tools can summarize accurately without misrepresenting
  the offer.
